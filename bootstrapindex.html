The User Experience of History

The fields of history, information architecture, and user experience have more in common than one might expect. All three require the distillation of information from the noisy to the usable. Historians are asked to look at the human past, and amidst this sea of information, distill what pieces are important, which ones are related, and how they explain things. They then must be able to communicate them, in a manner appropriate to their audience.

The filed of UX asks its practitioners to accomplish a similar task. In information architecture, a client will have an information problem, and they need to be able to make sense of it, organize and structure it, and put it together to tell a coherent story. The UX design ensures the clear communication of this information, and together the information that was once just a mess of noise is now usable and understandable.
